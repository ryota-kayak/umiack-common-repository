Would you like to experience "Ohanami" (cherry blossom viewing) from a kayak in the heart of Tokyo? We'll leisurely paddle through canals excavated during the Edo period, enjoying the beautiful cherry blossom tunnels and unique views of the city from the water. Even for beginners or those less confident in their physical strength, these calm, wave-free waterways offer a safe and enjoyable journey.

Tour Highlights

  1. [Stunning View] Cherry Blossom Tunnel on Water

    Experience the cherry blossoms blooming in full glory along the canals. You'll paddle under branches heavy with blossoms reaching toward the water and slice through carpets of floating petals. It's a surreal and magical perspective on the sakura season that you simply cannot find on land.

  2. [History] Time Travel through Edo Canals and Modern Tokyo

    Did you know such a place existed in central Tokyo? This is a hidden "urban wilderness" unknown even to many locals.
    These canals, originally built for logistics in the Edo period, offer a fascinating contrast between historic stone embankments and the modern skyscraper skyline. See a hidden side of Tokyo that has transformed from wetlands to Edo and finally into one of the world's leading metropolises.

  3. [Experience] Breakthrough the "Mini Panama Canal" by Kayak

    Koto-ku is a unique area situated below sea level, similar to parts of the Netherlands. To manage water levels between the Sumida River and the local canals, massive lock gates—often called the "Mini Panama Canal"—were built. Amazingly, you can pass through these locks in a kayak. The sheer scale and presence of the structure are truly breathtaking.

  4. [Pure Bliss] Freshly Roasted Specialty Coffee

    We prepare a special tea time featuring specialty coffee, roasted from green beans and brewed right before your eyes. Enjoy the ultimate luxury of coffee that is freshly roasted, freshly ground, and freshly brewed on the spot.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. I'm a beginner with limited stamina. Is that okay?
A. Yes! We paddle slowly through calm canals with no current, so it's a relaxed and safe experience even for those who aren't confident in their fitness.
Q. Is there a risk of capsizing?
A. Because the canals are very calm and shielded from wind and waves, the risk of capsizing is extremely low.

What to Bring / Clothing
  • Clothing that can get wet (avoid cotton products like jeans)
  • Shoes that can get wet or sandals with a heel strap
  • Hat (a brimmed sun hat recommended)
  • Rain gear (top and bottom) to protect against splashes and wind chill (even cheap vinyl ones are okay)
  • Gloves (standard ones are fine)
  • Change of clothes and a towel (store in a waterproof bag to ensure they stay dry)
  • Sunglasses (to protect against glare from the water)
  • Sunscreen
  • Snacks and drinks (you can eat and drink while kayaking)
  • Drop-prevention straps for phones and cameras (recovery is impossible if dropped in the water)
  • Please use waterproof bags for electronics and valuables to prepare for any accidental falls.
