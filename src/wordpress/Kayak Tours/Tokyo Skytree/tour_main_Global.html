Discover Tokyo's historic canal network, dating back to the Edo period. On this tour, you'll paddle through the quiet waterways of the city toward the iconic Tokyo Skytree. The journey takes approximately 3 to 4 hours. Depending on your preference and energy levels, we offer a simple "Out-and-Back Course" or a more comprehensive "Loop Course" to fully enjoy the urban scenery.

Tour Highlights

  1. [Unbeatable Views] Tokyo Skytree Up-Close

    As you paddle through the downtown canals, the Tokyo Skytree looms larger and larger. Eventually, it reaches a scale that barely fits in a smartphone frame, reaching a point so close you have to look straight up to see the top. Experience the overwhelming presence of this landmark from the unique perspective of the water.

  2. [History] The Intersection of Old Edo and Modern Tokyo Discover a "hidden water wilderness" in the heart of the city, largely unknown even to locals. As you travel along canals built for logistics during the Edo period, you'll see a stark contrast between historic stone embankments and modern skyscrapers. Decode Tokyo's hidden history—from a vast swampland to the megalopolis it is today—right from the waterline.
  3. [Water Mirror] The "Upside-Down Skytree" Reflection

    The inland canals are naturally shielded from the wind, resulting in calm, mirror-like water. This allows for a perfect "upside-down" reflection of the Skytree. From the low vantage point of a kayak, the real tower and its reflection seem to merge, creating a unique visual experience distinct from any view on land.

  4. [Perspective] Urban Space from Sea Level

    Because you are paddling on water lower than the surrounding ground level, you get a unique "bottom-up" view of the city in a tranquil silence isolated from the urban bustle. Compare the historic canal infrastructure with the surrounding neighborhoods and modern skyscrapers for a three-dimensional experience of both old and new Tokyo.

  5. [Pure Bliss] Freshly Roasted Specialty Coffee

    Enjoy a relaxing tea time with specialty coffee, roasted and dripped right before your eyes. Savor the ultimate luxury of coffee that is freshly roasted, freshly ground, and freshly brewed on the spot.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. I'm a beginner with limited stamina. Is that okay?
A. Yes! We paddle slowly through calm canals with no current, so even those who aren't confident in their fitness can enjoy the tour comfortably.
Q. Is there a risk of capsizing?
A. Since the canals are very calm without waves or currents, the risk of capsizing is extremely low.

What to Bring / Clothing
  • Clothing that can get wet (avoid cotton products like jeans)
  • Shoes that can get wet or sandals with a heel strap
  • Hat (a brimmed "sun hat" recommended)
  • Rain gear (top and bottom) to protect against splashes and wind chill (even cheap vinyl ones are okay)
  • Gloves (standard ones are fine)
  • Change of clothes and a towel (store in a waterproof bag to ensure they stay dry)
  • Sunglasses (to protect against glare from the water)
  • Sunscreen
  • Snacks and drinks (you can eat and drink while kayaking)
  • Drop-prevention straps for phones and cameras (recovery is impossible if dropped in the water)
  • Please use waterproof bags for electronics and valuables to prepare for any accidental falls.
